What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting information and systems from malicious attacks. It involves using technology, processes, and policies to safeguard computer systems and networks from unauthorized access, data loss, or damage. Individuals, businesses, and organizations need to have good cybersecurity practices to protect their data and systems from hackers, malware, and other threats.

Cyber attack

A cyber attack is an unauthorized system or network access by a third party or hackers. These attacks often lead to data breaches which can lead to data loss, manipulation, and financial loss.

Types of cyber attacks

1. Malware attack

The attacker uses malicious software viruses that disguise themselves as legitimate software, block access to network key components, and steal all confidential data without the victim's knowledge through Ads and dangerous links that a user clicks.

Prevention
  • Use antivirus software to protect your devices against malware.
  • Use firewalls to filter traffic entering your devices.
  • Be alert and avoid clicking on suspicious links.
  • Update your operating systems and browsers regularly.
    • 2. Phishing

    The attacker impersonates themselves as a trusted contact and sends out fake emails to the victim and by the victim opening the email or attachment the attacker gains access to private information and account credentials.

    Prevention
  • Update passwords regularly.
  • Use an anti-phishing toolbar
  • Set your devices to update software automatically
  • Back up your data for protection.
    • 3. Password attack

    A hacker cracks your passwords with certain programs and tools to access personal and confidential information from your devices.

    Prevention
  • Do not use the same password for multiple accounts.
  • Update your passwords regularly to limit exposure.
  • Use strong alphanumerical passwords with special characters.
  • Take caution to not use any password hints in the open.
    • 4. Man in the middle attack

    An attacker intercepts a two-party communication, eavesdrops, and hijacks the session between the host and client to steal or manipulate data.

    Prevention
  • Visit websites with secure connections.
  • Check for certificate warnings.
  • Be mindful of using public Wi-Fi networks.
  • Use a VPN to inscription data you send online.
    • 5. Crypto jacking

    This attack takes place when attackers have access to your device for mining cryptocurrency. Access is gained by infecting a website or making the victim click on malicious links.

    Prevention
  • Update software and all security apps.
  • Familiarize yourself with crypto-jacking to detect and prevent threats.
  • Install an Ad blocker on your devices.
  • Have extensions used to identify and block crypto mining scripts.
